    MS Dhoni Career-Wiki

    • Dhoni was chosen for the Central Coalfields Limited squad by Dhaval Shahay in 1998. This was a special day for Dhoni since he was about to play professional cricket, which he had never done before. Dhoni performed admirably in his team's matches, demonstrating his abilities, which attracted Dhaval Sahay, who used his contacts in the Bihar board to secure Dhoni's selection.
    • Dhoni made his ODI debut when India desperately needed a wicketkeeper-batsman combo and no one seemed to suit the bill. He was chosen for the Bangladesh tour, but he had a poor start and finished with a duck. Despite his dismal showing, he was chosen for a trip against Pakistan.
    • Dhoni took over as Test captain from Dinesh Karthik after impressing in ODIs in December 2005. In his second test match, he scored his first half-century.
    • He was chosen for a T20 international but did not play in 2006. But the following year, he was given the opportunity to lead a youthful Indian team to the World Cup, and he became just the second Indian captain to win the World Cup.
    • In the inaugural year of the Indian Premier League, he was picked by Chennai Super Kings for $1.5 million, making him the most expensive player. He guided his side to win in 2010, 2011, and 2018, and he won the Champions League in 2010 and 2014.
